The **Loftie Clock** is one of the few bedside devices specifically designed for people trying to keep their phones out of the bedroom. This review summarizes what verified buyers consistently report across major retailers and the manufacturer site, plus the design choices worth knowing before you buy.

<ProductCard productId="loftie-clock" badge="Editorial Pick" />

## What buyers consistently like

- **Two-phase alarm.** Verified reviewers frequently mention the gentle first chime followed by the actual alarm a few minutes later. It&apos;s a small detail with a noticeable effect on morning grogginess for many users.
- **Phone-free design.** The most cited reason for buying. Owners describe being able to charge their phone in another room without giving up their alarm or sleep sounds.
- **Curated content library.** Sleep stories, breathwork sessions, and ambient sounds — all without an algorithmic feed trying to keep you scrolling.

## What buyers consistently mention as drawbacks

- **App required for setup.** First-time configuration requires the Loftie app and an account. Once configured, the clock works independently.
- **No sunrise light.** Unlike the Hatch Restore 2, there&apos;s no gradual light wake-up. If a sunrise alarm is your main need, this isn&apos;t the right device.
- **Premium pricing.** At ~$165, it&apos;s more expensive than basic alarm clocks. Buyers consistently note that the value comes from heavy daily use, not occasional alarms.

## How it compares to the Hatch Restore 2

Both devices target the &quot;phone-free bedroom&quot; market but solve slightly different problems. The Hatch is the all-in-one option with a sunrise light, broader content library, and subscription tier. The Loftie is more focused — it doesn&apos;t do sunrise wake-ups, but it does the alarm and content experience without aggressive upselling.
