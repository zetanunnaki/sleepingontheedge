## What it is

- **Material:** Bamboo viscose (bamboo-derived rayon blend)
- **Weave:** Twill weave for softness and durability
- **Set includes:** 1 flat sheet, 1 fitted sheet, 2 pillowcases (queen)
- **Deep pocket:** Fits mattresses up to 16 inches

- **Colors:** 30+ colors including solids and seasonal options
- **Care:** Machine washable cold, tumble dry low
- **Price:** $39.99 (queen)

An important distinction: "bamboo viscose" is not the same as "100% bamboo." The bamboo plant is processed through a chemical viscose/rayon process that transforms the raw fiber into a textile. The result retains some of bamboo&apos;s natural properties — softness, moisture-wicking, breathability — but it&apos;s a manufactured fiber, not a natural one. The FTC has cracked down on brands labeling viscose as "bamboo" without clarification, and Bedsure is transparent about the processing.

## What buyers consistently like

### 1. Cooling that punches above the price

The most-cited positive in aggregated reviews. Buyers switching from cotton or microfiber consistently describe the Bedsure bamboo sheets as noticeably cooler — both to the initial touch and throughout the night. The bamboo viscose fiber doesn&apos;t trap heat the way polyester microfiber does, and it wicks moisture more efficiently than standard cotton.

Is it as cool as $180 eucalyptus lyocell? Verified buyers who have compared both say no — but the gap is smaller than the price gap suggests. For hot sleepers on a budget, the Bedsure delivers 60–70% of the premium cooling experience at 20% of the cost.

### 2. Silky-soft texture

The second most-praised quality. The bamboo viscose fiber has a naturally smooth surface that produces a silky hand-feel — softer than cotton percale, comparable to low-end sateen. Multiple reviewers describe being surprised by the softness at this price point, using words like "luxurious" and "hotel-quality" — terms typically reserved for sheets costing 3–4x more.

The softness improves slightly after the first 2–3 washes as the fibers relax.

### 3. Massive color selection

With 30+ colors available, the Bedsure lineup dwarfs premium brands that offer 3–5 options. Buyers who need to match specific bedroom aesthetics consistently praise the selection. This is a practical advantage that premium brands rarely match.

### 4. Deep pocket fitted sheet

The 16-inch deep pocket accommodates thick mattresses and mattress topper combinations that defeat many budget sheet sets. Verified buyers with 12–14 inch mattresses plus toppers specifically mention the Bedsure fitted sheet staying in place — a common failure point at this price tier.

### 5. Hypoallergenic properties

Bamboo viscose is naturally resistant to dust mites and bacteria — properties that survive the manufacturing process. Buyers with allergies or sensitive skin consistently report fewer reactions compared to cotton or microfiber sheets. While this isn&apos;t a substitute for medical-grade bedding, it&apos;s a meaningful practical benefit.

## What buyers consistently complain about

### 1. Pilling over time

The #1 complaint across all review platforms. After 3–6 months of regular use and washing, many buyers report small pills forming on the surface — particularly in high-friction areas where skin contacts the sheet. This is a structural limitation of bamboo viscose at this price point: the fiber is inherently softer and less abrasion-resistant than cotton or eucalyptus lyocell.

Mitigation: washing inside-out on gentle cycle and avoiding fabric softener (which weakens fibers) helps, but doesn&apos;t eliminate the issue entirely.

### 2. Not 100% bamboo

Some buyers feel misled by the "bamboo" label, expecting a fully natural fiber product and receiving a chemically processed viscose. The FTC labeling is accurate — these are "bamboo-derived rayon" or "bamboo viscose" — but the marketing emphasis on "bamboo" can create expectations of a natural material that the product doesn&apos;t fully deliver.

### 3. Wrinkles easily

Bamboo viscose wrinkles more than microfiber (which barely wrinkles at all) and comparably to cotton. Buyers who prioritize a smooth, wrinkle-free bed appearance find this frustrating — especially at a price point where microfiber alternatives stay crisp-looking with zero effort.

### 4. Color fading with washes

A notable minority of reviewers report color fading after 10–15 wash cycles, particularly with darker colors. Cold washing helps, but some color loss appears to be built into the price point. Premium bamboo and eucalyptus sheets at higher prices generally hold color better.
