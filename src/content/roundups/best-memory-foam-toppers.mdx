Most mattress complaints can be solved with a topper for under $100 — without replacing a $1,000+ mattress. The two categories that consistently dominate topper reviews are **gel-infused memory foam** (softer, contouring, budget-friendly) and **natural latex** (firmer, more responsive, more durable, more expensive). Pick based on what you want to fix.

This guide covers the memory foam topper that leads aggregated budget reviews and the natural latex topper that leads premium reviews — two different solutions to two different problems.

## When a topper makes sense

**Your mattress is too firm.** This is the #1 use case. A 2–3 inch topper can dramatically soften a firm mattress and fix pressure-point pain that's keeping you awake.

**Your mattress is getting old but still structurally sound.** A topper can extend a 5–7 year old mattress by 1–2 years. Not forever, but enough to delay a full replacement.

**You're hot and your mattress runs warm.** A gel-infused or latex topper can meaningfully reduce heat retention on an all-foam mattress.

**You want to test a softer feel before buying a new mattress.** A topper is 1/5 the cost of a new mattress and lets you see if a softer feel actually fixes your sleep complaint before committing.

## When a topper won't help

**Your mattress has structural damage.** Sagging, broken springs, visible compression — none of these are fixable with a topper. You need a new mattress.

**Your mattress is too soft.** Toppers generally soften firm mattresses, not firm up soft ones. There are firm latex "mattress boosters" that can slightly firm things up, but they're less reliable than replacing the mattress.

**You have severe back pain from a mattress issue.** A topper is an incremental improvement. If the problem is serious, don't waste money trying to bandage it — see a doctor and consider a new mattress.

## What to look for

**1. Thickness.** 2 inches is the minimum to meaningfully change feel. 3 inches is the sweet spot for most people. 4 inches is overkill for most but useful for very firm mattresses that need significant softening.

**2. Material type.**
- **Memory foam (traditional or gel-infused)**: conforms to body, good pressure relief, sleeps warmer than alternatives (gel helps but doesn't eliminate heat), cheaper.
- **Natural latex**: firmer, more responsive (bounces back faster), cooler, more expensive, lasts 10+ years.

**3. Density.** Memory foam density (in lb/ft³) affects durability. 3+ lb/ft³ is the threshold for a topper that holds its shape beyond 1–2 years.

**4. Certifications.** CertiPUR-US (for memory foam) and GOLS/Eco-Institut (for latex) certify the material has been tested for harmful chemicals.

**5. Fitted sheet compatibility.** Adding a topper increases the total mattress depth, which may require deeper-pocket fitted sheets.
