## What "bamboo sheets" actually means

This is where bedding marketing gets murky. When you see "bamboo sheets," you could be buying:

**1. Bamboo viscose (rayon).** The bamboo plant is chemically processed into viscose rayon fiber. This is what most "bamboo sheets" actually are. The resulting fiber has the cool, silky hand associated with bamboo bedding — but it's chemically rayon, not raw bamboo.

**2. Bamboo linen.** Mechanically processed bamboo fiber, much rarer and more expensive. Feels more like linen (crisper, more textured) than viscose.

**3. Bamboo blends.** Bamboo viscose mixed with cotton, polyester, or other fibers. Most budget "bamboo" sheets are blends — often 60–70% polyester, 30–40% bamboo viscose — which dilutes the benefits.

**4. 100% bamboo lyocell.** A cleaner-process version of bamboo viscose with similar feel and slightly better environmental credentials. More expensive, less common.

**For buying purposes, know this:** The hand-feel, cooling, and moisture-wicking properties come from the viscose rayon structure — whether it's derived from bamboo, eucalyptus (Tencel), or beech wood (modal). They all feel similar. "Bamboo" is a marketing category more than a materially distinct fiber.

## What to look for in a bamboo sheet set

**1. Fiber percentage.** Read the label. 100% bamboo viscose > a cotton-bamboo blend > a polyester-bamboo blend. Budget options are usually blends.

**2. Weave.** Bamboo viscose is typically woven in a sateen pattern, which contributes to the silky feel. Percale bamboo is rarer but crisper.

**3. Thread count.** Bamboo sheets are typically 300–500 thread count. Above 500 is marketing fluff; below 250 will feel thin and wear out faster.

**4. Deep pockets.** Check fitted sheet pocket depth before buying. Bamboo fabric has less stretch than cotton, so inadequate pockets are a common complaint.

**5. OEKO-TEX certification.** This certifies the fabric has been tested for harmful chemicals, which matters for viscose processing specifically (some cheap manufacturers leave residue).

## Bamboo sheets vs. cotton: when to choose which

**Choose bamboo sheets if:**
- You sleep hot or have night sweats
- You prefer a silky, smooth hand over crisp cotton texture
- You want moisture-wicking without polyester synthetic feel
- You have sensitive skin (bamboo viscose is gentler than many cottons)

**Choose cotton (specifically long-staple cotton percale) if:**
- You prefer a crisp, cool, slightly-textured hand over silky
- You want maximum durability (10+ year sheet sets are possible in premium cotton)
- You sleep in a cold climate year-round and don't need cooling
- You want to iron your sheets (bamboo doesn't iron well)

Most hot sleepers end up preferring bamboo; most cold sleepers end up preferring cotton. Try both if you're not sure — sheets are one of the categories where personal preference dominates.

## How to wash bamboo sheets

**1. Cold water.** Hot water degrades bamboo viscose fibers over time.

**2. Mild detergent.** Skip bleach, fabric softeners, and harsh detergents — they weaken the fibers and can strip the natural silkiness.

**3. Low heat drying.** Line drying is ideal; tumble dry low works fine. High heat is the #1 cause of premature bamboo sheet wear.

**4. Separate from zippers and rough fabrics.** Bamboo can snag on zippers, velcro, and rougher fabrics in the same wash load. Wash sheets with sheets only if possible.
