Shift work is the hardest thing you can ask a sleep system to handle. Your body wants to be awake during the day and asleep at night — and shift workers need the opposite. The research is unambiguous: shift workers have measurably worse sleep quality, more sleep-related health problems, and higher accident rates than the general population. But shift workers who build a deliberate sleep-environment stack can meaningfully close the gap. This guide covers the four products that consistently show up in aggregated shift-worker sleep reviews — one for each major problem.

## What shift workers need to solve

**1. Blocking daylight.** The biggest issue. You're trying to sleep while the sun is up, and your circadian system is aggressively alert because of that light. Target: total darkness.

**2. Masking daytime noise.** Your neighbors are awake. Trash trucks run. Kids play. Dogs bark. Target: consistent white noise.

**3. Protection against light leaks in unavoidable situations.** Hotels, travel, family visits, random bright mornings when curtains aren't enough. Target: a good sleep mask.

**4. Waking up reliably when you need to.** Your body wants to stay asleep during the "alerting" phase of shift transitions. Target: a smart alarm with light + sound.

This guide covers one product per problem. No individual product solves all four — the combination is what makes shift work survivable.

## How to actually use the stack

**Before your sleep period:**
- Dim the Hatch to warm amber 30 minutes before target sleep time.
- Close the blackout curtains fully.
- Put on the Manta mask.
- Start the Rohm on pink noise at a comfortable volume.

**During sleep:**
- The mask blocks any light that leaks around the curtains.
- The sound machine masks any noise that gets through the walls.
- The dark, cool, quiet environment simulates biological night.

**Wake up:**
- The Hatch fades up gradually from warm amber to cool daylight over 20–30 minutes before your target wake time.
- An optional sound alarm kicks in if the light alone isn't enough.

This combination is consistently reported in aggregated shift-worker reviews as the most impactful sleep investment they've made.
