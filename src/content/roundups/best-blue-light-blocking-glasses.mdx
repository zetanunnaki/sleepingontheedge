Blue light blocking glasses are one of the most over-marketed and most under-explained sleep products on the market. Most cheap pairs on Amazon have nothing to do with sleep — they&apos;re anti-glare glasses for office workers. This guide separates the categories, explains what the research actually says, and highlights two pairs that consistently lead aggregated buyer reviews in the sleep-focused niche.

## The research, briefly

Several published studies have looked at the effect of evening blue-light blocking on melatonin onset and sleep quality. Results are mixed but generally support the idea that filtering short-wavelength light in the hours before bed can modestly improve subjective sleep quality and shift melatonin onset earlier — particularly in people with significant evening screen exposure.

<Callout kind="science" title="Filter spectrum is what matters">
  &quot;Blue light blocking&quot; covers a wide range of products. Glasses
  that filter only a small slice of blue (the kind sold for office screen
  glare) have minimal evidence for sleep benefit. Glasses that block both
  blue and green wavelengths in the 400–550nm range have stronger published
  support — and they&apos;re always strongly amber or red tinted.
</Callout>

## What to look for

- **Strong tint, not subtle.** Quality sleep-focused lenses have a noticeable amber, orange, or red tint. Clear or barely-tinted lenses are office glasses, not sleep glasses.
- **Published spectrum data.** Reputable manufacturers publish lab data showing exactly which wavelengths the lens filters.
- **Wraparound or wide frames.** Light entering from the sides bypasses the lens. The shape matters as much as the lens.
- **Comfortable for an hour or more of evening wear.** If you don&apos;t actually wear them, they don&apos;t work.

## When to wear them

<Callout kind="tip" title="The 2-hour rule">
  Most published studies on evening blue blocking use a 2–3 hour pre-bed
  window. Putting them on right before bed has minimal effect; putting them
  on at 8 PM if you sleep at 11 PM is when most users notice a difference.
</Callout>
