Sleeping well during pregnancy is one of the most common complaints in the second and third trimesters — and it's also one of the most solvable. A well-designed pregnancy pillow addresses the three specific problems pregnant people face: **belly weight pulling the spine forward**, **hip pressure from side-sleeping on a firm mattress**, and **the official recommendation to sleep on the left side** (which can be uncomfortable without support). The right pillow can make the difference between lying awake for hours and actually getting 7–8 hours of sleep.

This guide covers the two pregnancy support pillows that consistently lead aggregated buyer reviews on Amazon and Walmart.

## What to look for in a pregnancy pillow

**1. Shape.** U-shaped pillows wrap around the entire body and support belly, back, and between-the-knees in one piece. C-shaped pillows are smaller and more targeted. J-shaped pillows are the compact compromise. U-shaped is the most-recommended for heavier trimesters because you can flip positions without repositioning the pillow.

**2. Fill.** Shredded memory foam, polyester fiberfill, and microbead are the three main options. Shredded foam is the most supportive; polyester fiberfill is the cheapest and softest; microbead has a beanbag-like feel that's polarizing.

**3. Cover washability.** Pregnancy is messy — sweat, leaks, drool. A removable, machine-washable cover is essential.

**4. Size vs. bed.** U-shaped pillows are big. In a twin or shared queen bed, they can push out a partner. Measure your bed before buying.

## What pregnancy sleep positions actually work

The general recommendation during pregnancy is to sleep on your **left side** in the second and third trimesters, which improves blood flow to the placenta. Side sleeping also reduces acid reflux and snoring, both of which get worse during pregnancy.

Avoid sleeping flat on your back in the third trimester — the weight of the uterus can compress the vena cava and reduce blood flow. Sleeping slightly inclined (using a wedge) or fully on your left side avoids this.
