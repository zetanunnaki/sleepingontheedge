If you've started researching magnesium for sleep, you've probably already learned the hard lesson: **the form matters more than the dose.** Most drugstore magnesium is magnesium oxide — the cheapest form, with the worst absorption and the most common GI side effects. Magnesium glycinate is the form most consistently recommended in aggregated sleep-focused reviews because it absorbs well, doesn't cause digestive upset, and has a calming effect attributable to both the magnesium and the glycine it's bound to.

This guide covers the two magnesium glycinate supplements that consistently lead aggregated buyer reviews on Amazon and Walmart.

## Why glycinate, not oxide or citrate

- **Oxide** — Cheapest, ~4% absorption, frequently causes loose stools. Avoid for sleep.
- **Citrate** — Decent absorption, but it's primarily marketed for constipation and has a laxative effect at higher doses.
- **Glycinate** — Chelated form, well-absorbed, gentle on the gut. The glycine component is itself a calming amino acid. This is the form most-recommended in aggregated sleep reviews.
- **L-threonate** — A newer form that has some research on crossing the blood-brain barrier more effectively. Expensive; mixed aggregated reviews.

For sleep specifically, glycinate is the standard recommendation across sleep-focused communities, and it's what the best-reviewed products in this category use.

## What to look for on a label

**1. Elemental magnesium dose.** This is the number that matters. A bottle may say "1,000 mg of magnesium glycinate" but only deliver 100 mg of elemental magnesium. Always look for the elemental number.

**2. Fillers and additives.** Cheap supplements often include unnecessary binders, artificial colors, and flavorings. Look for short ingredient lists.

**3. Third-party testing.** NSF, USP, or ConsumerLab verification indicates a product has been independently tested for label accuracy and contaminants.

**4. No proprietary blends.** If a label says "sleep complex 500 mg" without breaking down individual doses, you don't know what you're taking.

## Dosage and timing

Most sleep-focused reviews report 200–400 mg of elemental magnesium, taken 30–60 minutes before bed, as the sweet spot. Starting lower (100–200 mg) for the first week and ramping up if needed reduces the risk of any GI side effects.
