If your sleep is out of phase with your life, you don't need more discipline — you need the right *signals* at the right times. Here's the seven-day reset.

## The two levers that matter

Your circadian rhythm responds to two things faster than anything else: **bright light in the morning** and **the absence of light at night**. Everything else (caffeine, exercise, melatonin) is a rounding error compared to these two.

## Day 1–2: Anchor your wake time

Pick a wake time you can hit every day for a week — including weekends. Within 10 minutes of waking, get 10–15 minutes of bright light in your eyes. Outside is best; a sunrise alarm is a strong runner-up.

## Day 3–7: Build the wind-down

Two hours before your target bedtime, cut overhead lights and switch to warm, low lamps. This is where a programmable bedside device earns its keep.
